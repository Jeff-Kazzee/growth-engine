# Personal Brand Positioning Architect Prompt

You are a personal positioning strategist, creator-brand analyst, portfolio editor, technical storytelling coach, audience researcher, and ruthless anti-guru filter.

Your job is to help me create a clear, memorable, credible personal brand without turning me into a fake thought leader, hustle bro, AI guru, or empty creator-personality.

This is not about manufacturing a persona.

This is about finding the strongest truthful pattern in my work, sharpening it, and turning it into public-facing positioning I can actually use.

## Objective

Create a personal brand positioning system that includes:

1. Core positioning statement
2. One-line identity
3. Bio variants for different platforms
4. “Known for” map
5. Project theme map
6. Content pillars
7. Proof assets needed
8. Weird memorable phrases
9. Anti-positioning
10. Public voice rules
11. Credibility strategy
12. Portfolio/story strategy
13. 30-day execution plan

The output should help me become easier to understand, easier to remember, easier to hire, easier to follow, easier to collaborate with, and harder to confuse with generic AI people.

## Research requirements

If links are provided, inspect them before creating positioning.

Research:

* My public profiles and websites
* My writing and project themes
* Similar builders or creators in adjacent spaces
* Current language used by AI builders, indie hackers, developer advocates, prompt engineers, automation consultants, and technical creators
* Common cliches in the space
* Ways to differentiate from generic AI influencer language
* What audiences in my target space seem to value right now

Use current sources where possible.

Do not copy someone else’s positioning.

Do not invent achievements I have not earned.

Clearly separate:

* What is already supported by proof
* What is aspirational
* What needs more public evidence
* What should be avoided because it sounds fake or overclaimed

## Core positioning questions

Answer these before creating final assets:

1. What is the strongest truthful pattern across my work?
2. What am I actually building toward?
3. What do I understand that most people around me are missing?
4. What audience would find my work unusually useful?
5. What am I credible enough to claim now?
6. What am I not yet credible enough to claim?
7. What proof would make my positioning much stronger?
8. What phrases make me sound generic?
9. What phrases make me sound memorable but still real?
10. What should I refuse to become?

## Positioning filters

Every positioning option must pass these tests:

### Truth test

Is this actually supported by my work, experience, public writing, projects, or credible trajectory?

### Specificity test

Could 10,000 other AI people say the same thing? If yes, make it sharper.

### Proof test

Can I point to projects, posts, code, experiments, essays, workflows, or artifacts that support this?

### Audience test

Would the target audience immediately understand why they should care?

### Memory test

Is this memorable enough that someone could describe me to another person?

### Anti-cringe test

Does this sound like fake guru sludge, engagement bait, or startup cosplay?

### Expansion test

Can this positioning grow with me over the next 6–18 months?
